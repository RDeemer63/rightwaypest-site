<ServiceSection slot="section_3" heading="Plant Selection Built for Northeast Florida's Climate and Pest Resistance">

Every plant we specify earns its place by thriving in Northeast Florida's sandy soils and subtropical humidity while resisting the pest pressures common across Duval and St. Johns Counties. For groundcovers:

- Asiatic jasmine and perennial peanut (the latter fixes nitrogen naturally, reducing fertilizer inputs over time)

Our shrub palettes lean heavily on Florida natives: Simpson's stopper, Walter's viburnum, and coontie (a native cycad that is virtually pest-free). Dahoon holly, East Palatka holly, and Southern magnolia anchor our ornamental tree selections. Firebush, blanket flower, and muhly grass provide flowering interest without inviting problems.

We deliberately avoid known pest magnets. Indian hawthorn is prone to Entomosporium leaf spot in our climate. Oversheared ligustrum becomes a whitefly haven. We won't install either unless a client specifically requests them, and we'll explain the trade-offs upfront.

For homeowners in Ponte Vedra and Palm Valley, this approach means fewer chemical applications and a landscape that works with Northeast Florida's ecology rather than against it.

Jacksonville's flat, sandy terrain and a water table that often sits close to the surface make drainage the starting point of any hardscape project. We build paver patios, fire pit surrounds, and outdoor living areas with French drains, dry creek beds, and precise grading corrections underneath. A beautiful space that floods after a summer storm isn't worth the investment.

For riverfront and Intracoastal properties, we design retaining walls to manage grade changes and coordinate City of Jacksonville permitting when walls exceed four feet or the site falls within FEMA Zone AE flood areas along the St. Johns River.

Every irrigation layout we spec uses smart controllers paired with rain sensors to comply with St. Johns River Water Management District two-day-per-week watering restrictions. These systems deliver precise coverage without waste, keeping turf and plantings healthy through Northeast Florida's dry springs and saturated summers alike.

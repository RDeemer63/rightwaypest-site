<ServiceSection slot="section_3" heading="Tree Health Assessment and Diagnostic Process">

Every assessment follows a structured diagnostic protocol running 45 to 60 minutes per property. We begin with a full canopy inspection, looking for dieback patterns, chlorosis, and declining vigor. Then we examine the trunk and root flare for fungal conks, boring dust, and entry holes left by pests like southern pine beetles. Where internal decay is suspected, we use resistograph or increment borer testing to map what's happening beneath the bark.

Soil sampling around the root zone is critical in Jacksonville. Sandy, low-organic-matter soils frequently drive pH imbalances and nutrient deficiencies that weaken trees from below. Those soil results connect directly to our organic compost topdressing service, amending the root zone with compost to boost mycorrhizal activity and restore soil biology. This is especially important in Northeast Florida's high-moisture environment, where drainage and soil structure directly affect tree longevity.

For confirmed pest or disease issues such as bacterial leaf scorch, we use trunk injection systems for targeted delivery. Every assessment concludes with a written report including photo documentation and specific treatment recommendations.
